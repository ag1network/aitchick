Wi-Fi Keeps Dropping? Fix Guide for Broward County

Streaming freezes, Zoom calls cut out, or your Wi-Fi randomly disconnects in certain rooms? Unstable Wi-Fi is extremely common in apartments, condos, and townhomes across Broward County. Use the steps below to stabilize your connection and if you want someone to just handle it, I can optimize it for you remotely.

Common Causes of Unstable Wi-Fi

  • Modem or router overheating
  • Poor router placement (inside a cabinet, corner, or closet)
  • Interference from neighbors, microwaves, baby monitors, etc.
  • Old or low-end ISP gateways (Xfinity, AT&T, WOW!, etc.)
  • Weak signal in distant rooms
  • Misconfigured mesh Wi-Fi or extenders
  • Outdated router firmware

Step 1 ‚Äî Power-Cycle Modem and Router

This simple step fixes a big chunk of random Wi-Fi drops.

  1. Unplug your modem and router (or combo box) from power.
  2. Wait at least 20 seconds.
  3. Plug the modem back in first and wait until the lights stabilize.
  4. Then plug the router or mesh base back in.

Step 2 ‚Äî Improve Router Placement

Your router‚Äôs location is half the battle. Bad placement = bad signal.

  • Place it as close to the center of your home as possible.
  • Keep it off the floor and away from big metal objects.
  • Avoid hiding it in cabinets or behind TVs.

Step 3 ‚Äî Try 2.4 GHz vs 5 GHz

If you‚Äôre far from the router, the 5 GHz band might drop or slow down a lot. The 2.4 GHz band is slower but reaches further.

  • Use 2.4 GHz when you‚Äôre far from the router (better range).
  • Use 5 GHz when you‚Äôre close and want full speed.

Step 4 ‚Äî Forget and Re-Add the Wi-Fi Network

  1. On your device, open Wi-Fi settings.
  2. Tap your network and select Forget.
  3. Re-join it and carefully type the password.

Step 5 ‚Äî Check for Device-Specific Issues

Sometimes only one laptop or phone keeps dropping while others are fine.

  • Make sure the device has recent updates installed.
  • Restart the device completely.
  • Check for VPNs or security software that might be interfering.

When You Might Need a Tech to Look at It

If the Wi-Fi still drops after all this, it may be a deeper issue:

  • Signal noise from neighboring networks
  • Old or faulty modem/router hardware
  • Bad mesh node placement or configuration
  • Multiple access points fighting each other
  • Wrong channel width or channel selection
  • ISP signal problems into the building

Wi-Fi Keeps Dropping? Fix Guide for Broward County

Streaming freezes, Zoom calls cut out, or your Wi-Fi randomly disconnects in certain rooms? Unstable Wi-Fi is extremely common in apartments, condos, and townhomes across Broward County. Use the steps below to stabilize your connection, or reach out if you want someone to just take it over.

Common Causes of Unstable Wi-Fi

  • Modem or router overheating
  • Poor router placement (inside a cabinet, corner, or closet)
  • Interference from neighbors, microwaves, baby monitors, etc.
  • Old or low-end ISP gateways (Xfinity, AT&T, WOW! all do this)
  • Weak signal in distant rooms
  • Misconfigured mesh Wi-Fi or extenders
  • Outdated router firmware

Step 1 — Power-Cycle Modem and Router

This simple step fixes most random Wi-Fi drops.

  1. Unplug your modem and router (or combo box) from power.
  2. Wait at least 20 seconds.
  3. Plug the modem back in first. Wait until all lights look normal.
  4. Then plug the router or mesh base back in.

Step 2 — Improve Router Placement

Your router’s location is half the battle. Bad placement = bad signal.

  • Place it as close to the center of your home as possible.
  • Keep it off the floor, away from big metal objects.
  • Avoid hiding it in cabinets or behind TVs.

Step 3 — Try 2.4 GHz vs 5 GHz

If you’re far from the router, the 5 GHz band might drop or slow down.

  • Use 2.4 GHz when you’re far from the router (better range).
  • Use 5 GHz when you’re close and want full speed.

Step 4 — Forget and Re-Add the Wi-Fi Network

  1. On your device, open Wi-Fi settings.
  2. Tap your network and choose Forget.
  3. Re-join it and carefully type the password.

Step 5 — When You Might Need a Tech to Look at It

If the Wi-Fi still drops, it can be a deeper issue:

  • Signal noise from neighboring networks
  • Old or faulty modem/router hardware
  • Bad mesh node placement
  • Multiple access points fighting each other
  • Wrong channel width or channel selection
  • ISP signal problems coming into the building
